Here is a breakdown of what to show on the dashboard and how to structure your top bar:
1. Dashboard Additions (Beyond the Tiles)
While the tiles give you the "numbers," a dashboard needs Trends and Lists to be actionable.
For the Recruiter (The "My Work" View)
Daily Call Funnel: A small bar chart showing: Calls Made → Interested → Sourced. It helps them see their "conversion" rate for the day.
Upcoming Interviews: A list of candidates scheduled for interviews today/tomorrow.
Stagnant Applications: A list of candidates who haven't had a status change in >48 hours (to prevent "leakage" in the funnel).

For the TL/Manager (The "Team Health" View)
Recruiter Leaderboard: A simple ranking of who has the most "Sources" or "Interviews" this week. Great for team morale.
Job Load Balance: A chart showing which jobs are over-serviced and which are being ignored.
Yield Ratio: Which recruiter is actually closing vs. just sending a high volume of resumes.

For the Admin/Owner (The "Revenue" View)
Pipeline Value: A "Weighted Revenue" forecast. (e.g., if a candidate is at 'Offer' stage with a ₹10L CTC, that’s a potential ₹83k billing).
Client Concentration: A pie chart showing which clients are giving the most business vs. which ones are just wasting the team's time with "tough-to-fill" roles.

Center: Global Search
A search bar that handles Phone Number, Email, or Candidate Name. In a fast-paced agency, this is the most used tool.

Right Side: The Command Center (Role-Dependent)
Management (Dropdown - TL/Admin only):

Team Activity: Real-time feed of what recruiters are doing.

Job Allocation: Where you assign jobs to team members.

Summary Reports: The hierarchical reports we built.

One-click to add a New Candidate or New Job.

Profile/Settings: Personal info and password change.
